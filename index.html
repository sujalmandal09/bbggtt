Bhagavad Gītā 2.22

The Eternal Soul

वासांसि जीर्णानि यथा विहाय
नवानि गृह्णाति नरोऽपराणि ।
तथा शरीराणि विहाय जीर्णा-
न्यन्यानि संयाति नवानि देही ॥
vāsānsi jīrṇāni yathā vihāya
navāni gṛhṇāti naro'parāṇi |
tathā śarīrāṇi vihāya jīrṇā-
nyanyāni saṁyāti navāni dehī ||

Just as a person discards worn-out clothes and puts on new ones,

likewise, the embodied soul discards worn-out bodies and enters new ones.

About This Śloka

This profound verse from the Bhagavad Gītā (Chapter 2, Verse 22) explains the concept of the eternal soul. It uses the analogy of changing clothes to illustrate how the soul changes bodies.

Key concepts:

  • Dehī - The embodied soul that accepts material bodies
  • Vihāya - Discarding or leaving behind
  • Jīrṇāni - Worn-out or old things
  • Navāni - New things

This teaching helps us understand the eternal nature of the soul and the temporary nature of the physical body.
